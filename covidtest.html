Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between the tests?

We offer three different tests. The antibody test is $49 and will tell you if you have the immune system response to Covid19. This test will not tell you if you currently have covid but it will indicate if you have the immune response which occurs if you have had covid in the past or have had the vaccine. It is unable to differentiate between previous infection and vaccination. The rapid antigen test is $89 and will tell you if you currently have Covid19, it is between 88-96% accurate and is best for people who are symptomatic and in their first week of symptoms. It comes back in about 15-30 minutes. The Molecular RT PCR Test is $199, it is the gold standard of tests and is the most accurate option. It comes back in about 1-8 hours. The RT PCR Test detects the genetic material of covid-19 and is able to detect the virus even if there is only a small amount in your system.

I am traveling, what kind of test do I need?

The test required is dependent on the destination and method of travel. We recommend you check the specific country’s embassy or state’s government website and to check the specific airline’s website requirements to determine which test to obtain.

Does my insurance cover this?

We are out of network and cannot go through insurance, however, we can provide an invoice after the fact so that you may ask your insurance company for a refund. The refund is dependent on the insurance company and your plan.

Should I get tested?

We recommend getting tested if you

  • Have symptoms
  • Had close contact with someone who was diagnosed with covid-19 or had covid-19 symptoms
  • Were recently traveling
  • Are planning to attend a large event or visit someone at increased risk of severe covid-19 illness
  • Recently attended a large indoor gathering
Is it too early to get tested?

Covid19 has an average of 5-6 days for incubation meaning that a positive test will most likely occur 5-6 days after exposure.

Do you test children?

Yes, we are able to test all children six months and older.

If I have the Covid-19 vaccine, will I test positive for Covid-19?

The vaccines cannot give you Covid-19 or make you test positive for Covid-19. This is because the vaccines do not contain the virus that causes Covid-19.

How is antibody testing done?

We are able to provide an antibody test using a blood sample from a finger stick.

What does a negative antibody test result mean?

A negative result indicates that antibodies, the special proteins used to fight infections, have not been detected in your blood. This can mean that you have not had Covid-19 however, it could also mean

  • There has not been enough time since infection for antibodies to develop, or
  • Your body did not produce enough antibodies for the test to be able to detect them
